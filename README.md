🛒 Global Mega Store: Sales & Performance Analytics (2011-2014)

📌 Project Overview

This project delivers an end-to-end Power BI business intelligence solution for a global retail entity. The analysis covers a massive dataset of 19K+ customers and over $165M in revenue, focusing on the critical drivers of profitability, customer behavior, and regional growth.

The core objective was not just to visualize data, but to answer a strategic business mystery: "Why did net profit decline in 2014 despite a steady upward trend since 2011?"

🔍 The Four Pillars of Analysis

To provide a holistic view, the project followed the standard data analytics framework:

Descriptive (What happened?): Tracked revenue, net profit ($64.89M), and identified that 84% of revenue is driven by the B2C (Individual) segment.

Diagnostic (Why did it happen?): Investigated the 2014 profit dip by analyzing the correlation between sales volume, product pricing, and discount impacts ($5.6M).

Predictive (What will happen?): Utilized temporal trends to forecast potential sales trajectories based on historical seasonality.


Prescriptive (What should we do?): Recommended data-driven resource allocation and marketing spend based on high-performing regions and products.

🛠️ Technical Contributions

Data Modeling & ETL: Cleaned and transformed complex datasets using Power Query, ensuring integrity across regional and temporal dimensions.

Advanced DAX: Authored complex measures for YoY Growth, Revenue after Discount, and Profit Margin calculations.

Geospatial Analysis: Integrated interactive maps to track performance across North America, Europe, and the Pacific.

Visualization: Designed a user-centric dashboard with high "Data-to-Ink" ratio, focusing on key performance indicators (KPIs).

💡 Key Business Insights

The 2014 Profit Gap: While 2013 was a peak year ($25.87M profit), 2014 saw a shift. The analysis identified whether this was due to increased operational costs or a shift in product mix.

Product Performance: Identified the top-grossing products and analyzed whether their success was driven by High Sales Volume or Premium Pricing.

Color & Aesthetics: Analyzed the impact of product attributes (like Color) on sales volume to optimize inventory.

Regional Impact: Discovered how geographical trends significantly influence the sales of specific product categories (Sports Equipment vs. Clothing).

📈 Impact
By pinpointing the exact moment and reason for the 2014 trend shift, this project provides a roadmap for stakeholders to optimize marketing spend and inventory management for future cycles.
